Frequently Asked Questions

Regular maintenance should typically be performed every six months, but this may vary by equipment type and usage. Always refer to the manufacturer’s recommendations.

Basic steps often include visual inspection for wear and damage, testing operational functions, checking alignment and calibration, lubricating moving parts, and ensuring that safety and performance standards are met.

Predictive maintenance uses data analysis tools and techniques to detect anomalies and potential issues before they cause equipment failure. This minimizes downtime and reduces maintenance costs.

A routine maintenance report should include details of the inspection process, any issues found, actions taken to resolve issues, recommendations for future maintenance, and confirmation that equipment is safe to operate.

The lifespan of equipment depends on factors like usage, environment, maintenance, and quality of manufacturing. Regular inspections and maintenance records can help predict and extend the useful life of equipment.
