Frequently Asked Questions

A water softener is a device that reduces the concentration of calcium, magnesium, and other ions in hard water. It helps prevent scale buildup in plumbing and appliances, improves detergent efficiency, and extends the lifespan of your plumbing system.

To determine the right size, calculate the total hardness of your water and the daily household water usage. Multiply these two factors to get the grains per day requirement, and choose a water softener model that meets or exceeds this capacity.

While some homeowners with plumbing experience may be able to install a water softener, it is often recommended to hire a professional installer. This ensures the system is set up correctly, safely, and is working efficiently.

Water softeners require regular maintenance, such as replenishing the salt supply in the brine tank and occasionally cleaning the system. It's also important to check the settings and ensure the system is regenerating properly.

Softened water is generally safe to drink. However, if you are on a sodium-restricted diet, you might want to consider using potassium chloride as an alternative to salt, or you can install a reverse osmosis filter for the kitchen tap to reduce sodium content.
