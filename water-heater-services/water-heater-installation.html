Frequently Asked Questions

The size of the water heater depends on the number of bathrooms and people in the house. Generally, a family of four might need a 50-gallon tank if using a conventional storage water heater. It's best to consult with a professional to assess your specific needs.

Typically, it takes about 2 to 4 hours to install a new water heater, assuming there are no complications. However, the installation time may vary depending on the type and model of the heater and the condition of the previous installation setup.

Tank water heaters store a large amount of heated water ready for use, while tankless models heat water on demand, offering potentially endless hot water supply and often more energy efficiency. Tankless models typically have a higher upfront cost but often last longer.

While some may attempt to install a water heater themselves, it is recommended to hire a licensed professional. Incorrect installation can lead to safety hazards like gas leaks, carbon monoxide emissions, or electrical issues.

Regular maintenance includes annual flushing to remove sediment buildup, inspecting the anode rod, and checking for leaks or corrosion. Always refer to the manufacturer's instructions or hire a professional for detailed maintenance routines.
